Your Guide to Join


Welcome to the support page for Join, your all-in-one assistant for managing tasks and team projects with clarity and ease. This guide walks you through what Join is, what it offers, and how to get started.


What is Join?


Join is a visual project management application built around the kanban method. Developed by students at the Developer Akademie during an intensive web development program, it offers a modern, intuitive interface to help teams stay organized.

Kanban – a method originating from Japan – is based on managing work via visual boards. It’s all about improving flow, avoiding overload, and keeping priorities visible at all times. With Join, this concept is translated into a simple and effective digital tool for managing your daily tasks.

Please keep in mind that Join was created for learning purposes. It may not meet all standards required for large-scale commercial use. While we aim for a smooth experience, occasional bugs or downtimes may occur.


Getting Started


Navigating the Board


When you open Join, you'll land on your kanban board. This board is your workspace and includes four predefined columns: To Do, In Progress, Await Feedback, and Done.


Managing Contacts


To work with others, head over to the Contacts area. Click on “New Contact”, fill in the required details, and you're ready to assign tasks to your team members.


Creating Tasks (Cards)


Tasks are displayed as cards within the board. To add one, just click the plus (+) icon in the column where the task should start. Enter the task name, details, deadline, and assign the responsible team members.


Updating Task Progress


Move cards across the board as work progresses. Simply drag and drop the card into the appropriate column – easy and visual.


Removing Tasks


Finished a task or added one by mistake? You can either move it to “Done” or delete it. Note: Deleting is permanent and can’t be undone.


Tips for Success


For the best results, keep your board updated regularly and ensure your team follows the workflow. A well-maintained board means less confusion and better collaboration.
